Jijenge Community Slum Tour

Jijenge community organizes tours in the most underserved slums in Nairobi namely Kiamaiko, Madoya, Mradi, Mlango, and Ngomongo. Routinely many visitors tend to visit Kibera where every household is served by at least 5 Non-profit Organization. At the above slums you experience poverty unmasked, forgotten human beings and where progress has never been experienced.

The tour guides are mothers who were born and brought up in those slums, now taking care of their children and those left behind by their relatives, neighbors who died, got incarcerated or varnished without a trace.

The women will welcome you to their houses to see how they live and those that they take care of who are ravaged by the H.I.V scourge and other ladies.

Our tours are very safe as they have been embraced by the community. The security-guards are the reformed youths within the slums, these young men get some of the income and also have become role models to others who they encourage to stay away from the vices.

Areas Where You Will Visit

  • Thabiti young mothers a group of thirty-five women all living with H.I.V and some of their children in Kiamaiko Slum, these community champions who are leading the fight against stigmatization, discrimination and marginalization against people living with HIV in underserved populations.
  • Door to door home visit in the sprawling slum, where you will be welcomed and get to learn about their day to day lives.
  • Beadwork center where lives are being changed by appreciating what the slum dwellers can do, their potential, their capacity to innovate and their skills. A strategy geared towards tackling poverty by getting the slum dwellers into work and helping them to feed, clothe, educate and protect their families.
  • Appreciate the work of community health workers, on critical needs and actions for caregiving, here you will be able to experience firsthand how health care is being dispensed to the most underserved community
  • And much more..

Particulars

  • The slum tour starts at KFC Thika Road Drive Through; from there we will proceed to Thabiti Community Hall in Kiamaiko Slum. For those wishing to Mradi the meeting point will be at Ashaki
  • Transport is available to and from your hotel to the slum at a cost (depending on the number of people)
  • The tour is held in the morning and afternoon and takes 3 hours
  • The tour is held in small groups of 4-7 persons to ensure that you get maximum attention from our guides
  • Videography and Photography rule: Pictures are allowed at dedicated places after permission of the guides
  • We are always open to customizing the visit as per your request as long as it is ethical and within the rule of law
